About

Tavern Traveler is a 2D action rpg with rouge lite elements that focus on combat and exploration alike. The game's combat system is heavily inspired by Absolver made by Sloclap while trying to accomplish a similar graphics style between Dark Devotion made by Hibernian Workshop and Kingdom made by Thomas van den Berg and Marco Bancale but with a lower pixel resolution of 16 by 16 pixels.


Story

-- Deep in the mountains of the great desert Ryn lays a shrine better forgotten by mankind. --


As a member of the kings special unit called the cloaks you are tasked to investigate an incident that took place in one of Ryn's shrines. What you are about to discover never should have happened...


Gameplay

In Tavern Traveler, your journey starts at the coast of Yara and continues through its mainland until you reach Ryn. During your journey, you will travel between taverns that shelter you. The roads between are full of dangers, you have to overcome by fighting all kinds of foes.

In a typical action based game, the player performs attacks and attack combos depending on the weapon he is holding and what buttons he is pressing. Those attacks and combos are always the same depending on your weapon. However, in Tavern Traveler you can select from a set of moves represented as cards for a certain type of weapon deciding for yourself how your player moves while attacking, therefore creating your own flow and individual combat style. Each move comes with its special ability like breaking block, avoiding certain attacks, causing elemental damage and more. To use a card, you have to match its demanding attributes. Attributes are for example your constitution, strength, dexterity, etc...

At the start of the game you can choose a starting class which is giving you some starting attributes and a deck of random cards you can build your own attack flow from. Should you die, that's it. All that's left are the cards you didn't had in your build, waiting for the next cloak in the last tavern you visited. Your skills and attributes are lost forever. You can have multiple characters, but only ever play one of them. They can be switched at a tavern. An older character can be a teacher for a younger one, so even the older character dies, not all progress is lost.

I want this to change with my game Tavern Traveler and I want to take you along my journey.
